About Us

What is this project for?

LifeLink exists for one main purpose: to help hospitals that have patients in need of an organ find that organ quickly, by connecting them to a shared pool of registered donors and to other hospitals where organs are available in stock.

The project bridges the gap between supply and demand: when a hospital has a patient waiting, it can see which nearby hospitals have organs in stock and access full donor and health details—so that matching is fast, informed, and ethical.

When a person in need is in Hospital A, and a matching organ is registered or available at Hospital B, time and information are critical. Every hour can matter. This project provides:

  • A consent-based donor registry where donors submit their details and full previous health history (conditions, medications, allergies, surgeries).
  • Storage of donor information by hospitals, so that multiple hospitals contribute to and use the same pool of data—no single hospital holds all the information alone.
  • A way for any hospital in need to locate nearby hospitals with organs in stock (via the Live Organ map) and to access organ donor details and health information—only for authorised hospital use, not for the general public.
  • Options to check organ donor details, blood donor details, and to manage donor registration (consent form) and donations—all from one platform.

By making donor details and availability visible only to hospitals, we keep the process secure and professional while giving every consenting donor the chance to save lives.

LifeLink does not replace medical or legal procedures: it supports them. Final eligibility, consent confirmation, and allocation remain with treating teams and national authorities. Our role is to make the right information available to the right people at the right time.
